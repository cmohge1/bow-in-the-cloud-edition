Woodbridge 4/5th 1826 Esteemed Friend Not having heard anything more respecting the intended Publication I am not aware whether the design has been abandon'd, or whether its accomplishment may be so far advanced as to render the annex'd too late for insertion. If in time, and worth using, they are heartily at thy Service. I only wish I could have sent thee somewhat worthier of thy acceptance, but if these serve to show that I wish well to thy efforts, & the object they have in view one end is answered. In the first of the annexed trifles I have endeavor'd briefly to express some of those better feelings which I cannot but think the common dictates of Nature must excite in the unfortunate Slave Mother, in reference to her equally miserable Child. But as I believe instances occur, would they were more frequent, in which through the labours of Missionaries &c the Consolations of the Gospel are opened to those injured & devoted Beings, I have en- deavour'd in the second short article to embody some of the thoughts & feelings of a Converted Negro –– still, I hope, without compromise of the principle. Surely the sentiment contained in the concluding Verse must occur to such an one –– for in my view deplorable as is the Bondage of the unhappy Negro the Slavery of the Enslaver, the hopeless yoke of which he is the bigoted Victim, implies a thraldom yet not more dreadful –– Thine respectfully B. B.
