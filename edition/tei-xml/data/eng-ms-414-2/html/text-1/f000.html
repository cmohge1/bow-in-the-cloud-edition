Woodbridge 4/5 Mo Month 1826 Esteemed Friend Not having heard anything more respecting the intended Publication I am not aware whether the design has been standard, or whether its accomplishment may be so far advanced as to render this annex'd too late for insertion. If in time, and worth using, they are heartily at thy service. Sorry only, if it could have lent one somewhat worthier of the acceptance, but if they serve to show that I wish, well to the effort, the object thy have in view and end is answered. In the spirit of the annex'd trifle I have endeavor'd briefly, to express some of those better feelings which I conceive constitute the common character of true Christians & Saints of whatever name or profession in the unfortunate Slaves, Mother, in reference to her equally unfortunate Child. But as all believe in Slaves now, would they were more frequent & in which Humanity, the latter of Humanities be the consolation of the oppressed & are forced to Suffer injury & Death Being, I have en- deavour'd in the second short trifle to embody some of those humble feelings of a Negro Mother still. I did without the consciousness consciousness of the painful. Surely the sentiment contained in the concluding Verse must reach to such as one. For in my own apprehension as is the Company of Humanity, Sympathy & the Glory of the Endeavour, the Welfare & the Lot which he is the highest Victim, implies a Sanction not more dreadful. Thine respectfully B. B.
